Missingness Analysis — Why Are Genotypes Missing?

Pararge aegeria GWAS — chr3 8–14 Mb focal region — 88 samples, 33,194 variants

Key Finding

The missing genotypes are caused by low sequencing depth, primarily in samples from Pool-6 which suffered a sequencing failure. At 1.6–3.8× coverage, bcftools mpileup produces zero reads at many positions (Poisson sampling: P(0 reads) = e−λ ≈ 10–20% when λ = 2×), resulting in missing genotype calls (./.). The --geno 0.2 PLINK filter retained variants with up to 20% missingness, and --mind 0.1 removed the 6 worst samples but left 2 borderline low-coverage samples (nr65, nr88) in the dataset.

The Mechanism: Low Depth → Missing Genotypes

How the variant calling pipeline creates missing data:
  1. bcftools mpileup requires reads with mapping quality ≥20 and base quality ≥20
  2. At low coverage (1–4×), many positions have zero qualifying reads for a given sample
  3. bcftools assigns these positions a missing genotype (./.:0,0,0:0:0,0, i.e. DP=0)
  4. The site-level filter (QUAL>20 && INFO/DP>5) checks total depth summed across all ~94 samples, not per-sample — so variants pass even when individual samples have DP=0
  5. PLINK’s --geno 0.2 allows up to 17 of 88 samples to be missing per variant

This is not a region-specific problem. The missingness rate in chr3 8–14 Mb (~27.3%) is comparable to the genome-wide average. The grey cells in the genotype plot are a genome-wide pattern driven by sample depth, not by any feature of this chromosomal region.

The NN group has slightly higher mean missingness, driven by nr65 (8.0%) and nr88 (4.6%), both low-coverage NN samples. This difference is not statistically significant and should not bias the GWAS.

Recommendations

  1. Consider removing nr65 and nr88 from the analysis — they are borderline low-coverage samples that contribute disproportionately to missingness
  2. Tighten --geno to 0.05 or 0.10 if cleaner genotype plots are desired (this removes variants with >5% or >10% missing samples)
  3. For future experiments: implement post-sequencing QC to catch pool failures early (flag any sample with mean depth <5× before variant calling)
  4. Consider per-sample DP filtering during variant calling: set individual genotypes to missing when DP < 3, then apply a stricter --geno threshold
